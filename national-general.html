National General Insurance

At National General Insurance, I worked one on one with the senior architect to design and develop a website for internal administrative purposes. Their goal was to have a user-friendly location for less tech-savvy members of the organization to view customer information and orders. Through an iterative process of design, I delivered a product that could cleanly and efficiently display records for the tens of thousands of customer orders in the database.

The Process

The work environment at National General Insurance was somewhat more relaxed and informal than I was previously accustomed to, and I was allowed almost complete creative freedom to decide the appearance of the site as well as the frameworks to use. Though the project was initially intended to be completed in AngularJS, given the relative age of that framework combined with the rapid advancements in Angular, I successfully campaigned to use Angular 2 instead, as I believed that it would be supported for a longer period of time and degrade more gracefully.

For several months, I worked directly under the senior architect to design, write, and integrate the Angular frontend of the product with the REST API for the SQL Server. For this project, I primarily worked with Angular 2, C#, Bootstrap, HTML, and CSS, and did some minor work with the SQL Server. I conducted end-to-end testing to ensure the product's reliability and efficiency when dealing with large datasets.

Technologies Used

On the backend, the administrative site is a C# project built with Apache Maven, with a Microsoft SQL Server database. It uses Angular 2, HTML5, CSS, and Bootstrap for design.
